How our art is made


We at MOMA appreciate having fun with acronyms and the title of our gallery is the foremost example. Indeed, the art displayed here is made from cracked monitor screens, hooked up to a testing program which displays a flat primary-color image. Photographs are taken and carefully edited to produce an artistic image that we and our viewers will enjoy looking at, and perhaps with complexities yet to be discovered and contemplated.


On collection titles


Our various collections are also given initialism titles, such as "OMA", "MAAT", "ROMA", and more. Some of these are meant to satire other significant titles with the same initials, but others are simply abbreviations. We encourage our viewers to try to determine the actual titles of our abbreviated collections - the first person to guess the name of any collection will be granted the right to name one work from an upcoming collection. If you'd like to submit a guess, feel free to contact us!
